I obtained my B.Tech degree in Mathematics and Computing from Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati.

I am currently trying to leverage the rich structure of theories from topological manifolds, algebraic curves, surfaces, and functional analysis, in building robust and creative models for analyzing data.

I recently worked as a researcher in Advanced Technology Lab - Multimedia Division at Samsung Research Institute, Bangalore, India. Here, I developed a Deep Hierarchical Network invariant to rotation of objects in the input image and extensively studied a technique based on contraction mappings to increase the lower bound on the margin in the input space of a non-linear classifier (in constrast to SVMs which maximize margin in the feature/kernel space). The motivation was to get high generalization across similar datasets when the parameters are estimated using only a single dataset. Also, I implemented video temporal segmentation as a core component of video summarization, based on Affinity Propagation and Sequential Determinantal Point Process. Please check the publications page for more information.

My past work experience has been with Microsoft Research, India, where I was a research intern in the CISL (Cloud and Information Services Lab) group. Here, I worked on the problem of real-time detection of issues in high-dimensional performance counter time series data with the aim of detecting the time of the issue and the subset of counters which caused the issue. Please check the projects page for more information.
