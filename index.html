I am a researcher in Advanced Technology Lab - Multimedia Division at Samsung Research Institute, Bangalore, India. My work mainly concerns with developing statistical models with better generalization across different high-dimensional datasets, for applications in the domain of computer vision. I obtained my B.Tech degree in Mathematics and Computing from Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati.

My past work experience has been with Microsoft Research, India, where I was a research intern in the CISL (Cloud and Information Security Lab) division. There, I worked on the problem of issue detection in high dimensional time series data using sparse dictionarly learning, sparse coding and clustering techniques. Please check the research page for more information.

Before Microsoft, I remotely worked on a project with INCF (International Neuroinformatics Coordinating Facility) through Google Summer of Code 2014. In this project, I developed a tracing software for the real-time vectorization of brain atlases. Here is a link to this project's page.
