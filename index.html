I am a researcher in Advanced Technology Lab - Multimedia Division at Samsung Research Institute, Bangalore, India. Here, my work mainly concerns with building robust statistical models with better generalization across similar high-dimensional datasets for applications in the domain of computer vision and pattern recognition such as video summarization, object classification and action recognition. I obtained my B.Tech degree in Mathematics and Computing from Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati.

My interests are broadly in the field of robust statistics, reasoning, attention and memory based machine learning models, reinforcement learning and spatio-temporal clustering. Currently, we are looking into developing deep hierarchical nonlinear models with high margin in the high-dimensional input space and with invariance to rigid-transformation as well as small deformation of the data and how well they generalize across similar datasets when the parameters are estimated using only a single dataset.

My past work experience has been with Microsoft Research, India, where I was a research intern in the CISL (Cloud and Information Services Lab) group. There, I worked on the problem of real-time detection of issues in high-dimensional performance counter time series data with the aim of detecting the time of the issue and the subset of counters which caused the issue. Please check the projects page for more information.
